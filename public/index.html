My Work

What do I do

Here's a little rundown of the services I provide.

websites

In todays world, Your website is highly likely to be the first point of contact with Your clients. It is obvious You'll want to make the best first impression possible. With the amount of options out there, you want your site to be performant, good looking, and easy to navigate. I can help with that.

Apps

Whenever a website responds to users behaviour, it can be called an app. Broadly speaking. Apps can be anything from a simple loan calculator, through property finder, all the way to social media sites. Whether you need a small Single Page Application to help your clients navigate through your service or product portfolio, or you need a sophisticated e-commerce site, feel free to get in touch.

Backend Development

Unlike simple apps that live solely on users machine, most applications actually need a server to live on, usually with a database connection. Although I specialise in Frontend Development, I can also help with setting up Your server-side logic. Please get in touch so we can discuss Your exact needs.

How does the magic happen

If you're interested in my work process, I outline a simplified version below.

  • 1.

    Every successful project starts with a clarity of the goals. What is the desired outcome? Is it to increase your customer base? Do you need broader outreach? Or do you need a killer landing page to drive your sales through the roof?

  • 2.

    Once we've established the purpose of the end product, the next step is drawing up a design. I always outsource this part to one of my associates who specialises in design, as they will be able to deliver a far superior result than me.

  • 3.

    Once the designs are done and hashed out, it's time to decide what procedure will I use for getting the job done. Some projects will work better created via Functional programming paradigm, some deliver better results when done via Object Oriented Programming. Using the right tools for the job is half the success.

  • 4.

    Now that everyhing is ready, it's time to write the code. Whenever possible, I like to get a draft with just simple markup and style reviewed by the client, so they can decide on any changes before I start writing all the logic.

  • 5.

    Now for the real fun part. Giving the website or app its behaviour is one of the most fun jobs on Earth. The process can be often frustrating, but the feeling when all the pieces fall into place is like no other.

  • 6.

    Once all the coding is done, there is always some back and forth communication with the client, and potentionally a backend developer, if the site/app requires it. Once everything is ready to be handed over, I like to bundle everything to increase performance, test for any undesired behaviour, and if everything works and looks great, deploy.
